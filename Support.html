It's alright to want help

You are not alone, and we want to help.

We understand the importance of reaching out for support when facing mental health challenges. Below are several helplines that provide assistance, guidance, and resources for individuals in need.

Lifeline Australia: Lifeline Australia offers 24/7 crisis support and suicide prevention services. Their trained counselors provide confidential and non-judgmental support to individuals experiencing emotional distress, loneliness, or mental health concerns. Lifeline Australia aims to empower individuals to find hope, connection, and strategies to cope with life's challenges. Visit their website at https://www.lifeline.org.au/ for more information.

Headspace: Headspace is a national youth mental health foundation in Australia. They provide online and phone support to young people aged 12-25, covering a wide range of mental health issues. Headspace offers counseling, information, and resources to support the well-being of young individuals. Visit their website at https://headspace.org.au/online-and-phone-support/ for more information.

Kids Helpline: Kids Helpline is Australia's only free, private, and confidential 24/7 helpline specifically for young people aged 5-25. They provide counseling and support for a range of issues, including mental health, bullying, family problems, and more. Kids Helpline aims to empower young individuals to overcome challenges and build resilience. Visit their website at https://kidshelpline.com.au/ for more information.

Beyond Blue: Beyond Blue is a reputable mental health organization in Australia that provides support, information, and resources for individuals experiencing anxiety, depression, and other mental health conditions. They offer a helpline, online chat, and a wealth of resources for individuals and their families. Visit their website at https://www.beyondblue.org.au/ for more information.

BetterHelp: BetterHelp is an online counseling platform that connects individuals with licensed therapists via secure video, phone, chat, or messaging. They offer convenient and accessible mental health support, allowing individuals to seek help from the comfort of their own homes. Visit their website at https://www.betterhelp.com/get-started/ for more information.
